More than a maritime robot

Guardian is a sovereign maritime AI and data platform that happens to be embodied in autonomous, semi-submersible hardware. It provides a persistent, covert sensing fabric across harbours, chokepoints, and Arctic seaways, integrating directly into national command, control, and intelligence systems.

Sovereign by design

Air-gapped, nation-hosted data infrastructure that keeps sensitive maritime intelligence under national control, from raw sensor data to AI-delivered insight.

Embodied AI at the edge

Semi-submersible sentinels with mast-mounted ELINT/SIGINT and EO/IR payloads, running Guardian autonomy stacks to detect, classify, and cue responses in real time.

Integrated into the defence stack

Built to complement aerospace ISR and cyber operators as the maritime layer in the new defence stack—feeding secure data into existing C2 and fusion centres.

From ocean sensors to sovereign cloud

Guardian is architected as a full-stack maritime infrastructure platform: persistent submersible edge nodes, secure maritime mesh, and a sovereign AI cloud for pattern-of-life analysis and targeting support.

Edge layer — autonomous sentinels

Semi-submersible sentinels engineered for covert, long-endurance missions in contested waters and extreme climates.

  • 72-hour uncrewed endurance in maritime environments.
  • Semi-submersible profile for reduced radar and visual signature.
  • Configurable payload bays for ELINT/SIGINT, AIS, radar, EO/IR, and mission-specific kits.

Network layer — maritime mesh

Resilient, policy-aware connectivity from edge to command centre across coastal, harbour, and Arctic operating areas.

  • Multi-bearer comms across satellite, line-of-sight, and secure RF.
  • Data routing tuned to classified, sensitive, and unclassified streams.
  • Secure integration with existing maritime and joint C2 systems.

Cloud layer — maritime AI

Sovereign maritime AI that turns sensor feeds into pattern-of-life intelligence, anomaly detection, and decision support.

  • AI-assisted pattern-of-life and anomaly detection across key routes.
  • Threat scoring and cueing for manned air, surface, and shore-based assets.
  • Operator-centric interfaces for navies, coast guards, and law enforcement.
