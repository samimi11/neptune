Building Canada’s first publicly listed maritime defence neo‑prime, delivering persistent, covert surveillance and response without putting sailors at risk.

Neptune Sentinel • Vancouver, BC • June 2026

Mission & vision

Mission. Deploy semi‑autonomous, harbour‑safe submersible vessels that extend Canada’s maritime domain awareness and response across ports, coastal approaches, seaways, and the Arctic—with human operators remaining on shore or over‑the‑horizon.

Vision. Become the sovereign maritime layer in a new generation of defence neo‑primes—complementing aerospace and cyber stacks with a purpose‑built, blue‑water and littoral robotics company.

What we build

  • Multi‑modal submersible surveillance vessels.
  • Signals intelligence and FLIR sensor payloads.
  • Autonomy and command‑and‑control software.
  • Interfaces for kinetic and non‑kinetic effectors.
Slide 2 – Mission & vision

Why now – macro drivers

  • Accelerating grey‑zone maritime activity: smuggling, sanctions evasion, hostile state research vessels, and uncrewed intrusions in NATO ports and critical waterways.
  • Persistent under‑investment in Canadian maritime infrastructure versus air and land, leaving gaps in ports, coastal chokepoints, and the Arctic.
  • Severe manpower constraints across Navy, Coast Guard, and law enforcement—traditional crewed patrol craft cannot scale with mission load.
  • Rapid adoption of autonomous systems and AI‑driven ISR across NATO, with new budget lines specifically for uncrewed and semi‑autonomous maritime platforms.
  • Growing emphasis on sovereign, air‑gapped defense compute and classified data processing—favouring vendors who design for disconnected operations from day one.
  • No publicly listed Canadian maritime defence prime; capital markets and defence buyers are both looking for a scalable, focused platform company.
Slide 3 – Why now

Operational problem

Today’s maritime patrol reality

  • Harbours and chokepoints rely on a patchwork of cameras, AIS receivers, and sporadic manned patrols.
  • Coastal and Arctic patrols require expensive crewed vessels operating in harsh, low‑yield conditions.
  • Signals intelligence in the maritime domain is episodic—small windows when manned assets are present.

Resulting capability gaps

  • Limited pattern‑of‑life understanding for vessels and crews transiting harbours and coastal routes.
  • Inability to maintain covert, persistent presence close to high‑risk targets without escalating visibly.
  • Insufficient coverage of Arctic and remote seaways where human exposure and cost are high.
Slide 4 – Problem

Solution – Neptune sentinel

Neptune Sentinel is a multi‑modal submersible semi‑autonomous surveillance vessel designed to patrol harbours, seaways, rivers, coastal zones, and Arctic waters.

  • Operates in fully autonomous, semi‑autonomous, or man‑in‑the‑loop modes depending on mission and ROE.
  • Semi‑submersible: inflates to surface to employ FLIR and EW payloads; submerges for covert transit and data collection.
  • Built around a rugged hard shell encased in harbour‑safe Zodiac‑style inflatable material.

Primary mission set

  • Harbour and port patrol.
  • Coastal and seaway monitoring.
  • Arctic and remote‑water sovereignty patrols.
  • Search and rescue cueing and overwatch.
