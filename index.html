Executive Summary

Neptune addresses a critical sovereign vulnerability: uncompromised coastal surveillance across blue-water, littoral, and harsh Arctic domains[cite: 1, 5].

The Mission

To deploy autonomous, semi-submersible platforms capable of executing relentless surveillance and data integration workflows across contested environments[cite: 3].

Platform Focus

Threat Landscape

The Maritime Capability Gap

Traditional crewed patrol crafts are unable to scale effectively against asymmetric grey-zone threats and escalating territorial incursions[cite: 10, 14].

Current Operational Realities

  • Patchwork Surveillance: Strategic harbors rely heavily on static cameras, simple AIS trackers, and random crewed rounds[cite: 16].
  • Prohibitive Arctic Costs: High-exposure environments require multi-million dollar traditional vessels operating in extreme climates[cite: 17, 21].
  • Episodic Intelligence: Signals collection suffers from massive blindspots, occurring only when crewed assets are physically stationed[cite: 18].

Critical Vulnerabilities

  • No continuous, long-term operational pattern-of-life visibility for vessels moving through vital straits[cite: 19].
  • An inability to maintain a persistent, covert presence near suspect hulls without creating standard military diplomatic flare-ups[cite: 20].
  • Severe military, coastal enforcement, and naval manpower deficits that severely limit standard fleet capacity[cite: 14].
