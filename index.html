Operational problem
04

Ports and chokepoints are blind in between patrols

Current reality

  • Patchwork of cameras, AIS receivers, and sporadic manned patrols.
  • Signals intelligence is episodic – small windows when manned assets happen to be on station.
  • Limited pattern‑of‑life understanding along key coastal routes.

Resulting gaps

  • No way to maintain covert, persistent presence near high‑risk targets without visible escalation.
  • Insufficient coverage of Arctic and remote seaways where human exposure and cost are high.
  • Slow, fragmented data when incidents cross jurisdictional boundaries.

Introducing Neptune Sentinel
05

A semi‑submersible autonomous sentinel for the world’s hardest waters

Neptune Sentinel is a multi‑modal, semi‑submersible surveillance vessel designed to patrol harbours, seaways, rivers, coastal zones, and Arctic waters – autonomously, persistently, and covertly.

Primary mission sets

  • Harbour & port patrol.
  • Coastal & seaway monitoring.
  • Arctic & remote‑water sovereignty patrols.
  • Search & rescue cueing and overwatch.

Core design

  • Modes: fully autonomous, semi‑autonomous, or man‑in‑the‑loop depending on ROE.
  • Semi‑submersible profile: surface for FLIR/EW; submerge for covert transit and collection.
  • Rugged hard shell with harbour‑safe inflatable collar for safety and durability.
