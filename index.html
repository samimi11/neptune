Why now
02

Maritime grey‑zone conflict is outpacing legacy fleets

Structural pressures

  • Rising grey‑zone activity: smuggling, sanctions evasion, hostile state research vessels, and uncrewed intrusions in NATO ports and critical waterways.
  • Persistent under‑investment in Canadian maritime infrastructure versus air and land – especially in ports, coastal chokepoints, and the Arctic.
  • Severe manpower constraints across navies, coast guards, and law enforcement; crewed patrol craft do not scale.

Technology & policy tailwinds

  • NATO is rapidly adopting autonomous and AI‑driven ISR with dedicated budgets for uncrewed and semi‑autonomous platforms.
  • Sovereign, air‑gapped defence compute is now a requirement, not a luxury.
  • Canada has no publicly listed maritime defence prime – capital markets and defence buyers are looking for a scalable, focused platform company.
The timing is ideal to create a public Canadian autonomous maritime champion.
Operational problem
03

Ports and chokepoints are blind in between patrols

Current reality

  • Patchwork of cameras, AIS receivers, and sporadic manned patrols in harbours and key chokepoints.
  • Signals intelligence is episodic – small windows when manned assets happen to be on station.
  • Limited pattern‑of‑life understanding for vessels, crews, and cargo transiting coastal routes.

Capability gaps

  • No way to maintain covert, persistent presence near high‑risk targets without visible escalation.
  • Insufficient coverage of Arctic and remote seaways where human exposure and operating costs are high.
  • Fragmented, delayed data when incidents cross jurisdictional boundaries.
Introducing Neptune Sentinel
04

A semi‑submersible autonomous sentinel for the world’s hardest waters

Neptune Sentinel is a multi‑modal, semi‑submersible surveillance vessel designed to patrol harbours, seaways, rivers, coastal zones, and Arctic waters – autonomously, persistently, and covertly.

Primary mission sets

  • Harbour and port patrol.
  • Coastal and seaway monitoring.
  • Arctic and remote‑water sovereignty patrols.
  • Search and rescue cueing and overwatch.

Core design

  • Operates in fully autonomous, semi‑autonomous, or man‑in‑the‑loop modes depending on mission and ROE.
  • Semi‑submersible profile inflates to surface for FLIR and EW payloads; submerges for covert transit and data collection.
  • Rugged hard shell encased in harbour‑safe Zodiac‑style inflatable material for safety and durability.
