Vision. Become the sovereign maritime layer in a new generation of defence neo‑primes—complementing aerospace and cyber stacks with a purpose‑built, blue‑water and littoral robotics company.

What we build

  • Multi‑modal submersible surveillance vessels.
  • Signals intelligence and FLIR sensor payloads.
  • Autonomy and command‑and‑control software.
  • Interfaces for kinetic and non‑kinetic effectors.

Operational problem

Today’s maritime patrol reality

  • Harbours and chokepoints rely on a patchwork of cameras, AIS receivers, and sporadic manned patrols.
  • Coastal and Arctic patrols require expensive crewed vessels operating in harsh, low‑yield conditions.
  • Maritime SIGINT is episodic—small windows when manned assets are present.

Resulting capability gaps

  • Limited pattern‑of‑life understanding for vessels and crews transiting harbours and coastal routes.
  • Inability to maintain covert, persistent presence close to high‑risk targets without escalating visibly.
  • Insufficient coverage of Arctic and remote seaways where human exposure and cost are high.
Slide 5 – Problem

Solution – Neptune sentinel

Neptune Sentinel is a multi‑modal submersible semi‑autonomous surveillance vessel designed to patrol harbours, seaways, rivers, coastal zones, and Arctic waters.

  • Operates in fully autonomous, semi‑autonomous, or man‑in‑the‑loop modes depending on mission and ROE.
  • Semi‑submersible: inflates to surface to employ FLIR and EW payloads; submerges for covert transit and data collection.
  • Built around a rugged hard shell encased in harbour‑safe inflatable material.

Primary mission set

  • Harbour and port patrol.
  • Coastal and seaway monitoring.
  • Arctic and remote‑water sovereignty patrols.
  • Search and rescue cueing and overwatch.
Slide 6 – Solution overview

Platform design

  • Hull. Internal hard shell for structural integrity and payload mounting, surrounded by an inflatable collar for harbour‑safe operations and collision tolerance.
  • Semi‑submersible. Operates primarily below the surface to avoid navigational conflicts, surfacing or inflating only when required for specific sensors or comms.
  • Power. High‑density battery system with solar recharging on upper surfaces for persistent loiter capability.
  • Propulsion. Low‑noise electric drive optimised for harbour environments and shallow waters.
  • Modularity. Open payload bay and mast interfaces for mission‑specific sensor or effector packages.
  • Safety. Designed to comply with harbour master and maritime safety regulations; programmable exclusion zones and depth limits.
Slide 7 – Platform design

Sensor & payload suite

Signals intelligence

  • Cellular interception and IMSI‑catcher‑type capabilities for crew device correlation.
  • AIS receiver and spoof‑detection logic for vessel identification and anomaly spotting.
  • Rogue cell‑tower and RF spectrum monitoring to flag illicit or covert communications.

ISR & effects

  • FLIR and EO cameras on a semi‑submersible mast for day/night visual surveillance.
  • Optional kinetic and non‑kinetic payload interfaces for deterrence, tagging, or interdiction.
  • Acoustic and environmental sensors to support navigation, SAR, and subsea infrastructure monitoring.
